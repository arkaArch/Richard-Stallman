Dr. Richard Stallman

The man behind the free software foundation

Here's a time line of Stallman's life:

  • 1953 - Born in New York, U.S.
  • 1974 - Earned a bachelor’s degree in physics from Harvard University
  • 1971 - Begun working at the Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (later Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence Lab) at the MIT. There he wrote the Emacs text editor in the C computer programming language with James Gosling (who later developed Java).
  • 1983 - Began working in his personal time on his GNU Projet.
  • 1985 - Stallman created the nonprofit Free Software Foundation, which initially focused on supporting his GNU Project.
  • 1990 - He was awarded a MacArthur fellowship, the so-called “genius award” that gives recipients a substantial financial stipend with no strings attached.
  • 1999 - Stallman published “The Free Universal Encyclopedia and Learning Resource,” a paper calling for the creation of an open-source encyclopaedia.
  • 2019 - Stallman resigned from MIT and the Free Software Foundation.
